Overview
A shepherd's crook of the Central Asian steppe, ancient and nomadic in origin, awakened mid-Storm from a museum archive where she had spent several undignified decades behind glass. She emerged already knowing how to walk, already looking for something to guide home. Spirit Afflatus. Unexpected carry. The crook still in her hand.
Personality
Curious and wandering — she followed flocks across wide steppe country for centuries and has not stopped being interested in what's on the other side of things. She meets everything new with genuine open warmth. The backbone surfaces when it needs to. She is not loud about it. She simply becomes very clear, very still, and very firm.
"Not that way. This way. It's alright — I'll show you."
Backstory
She remembers the animals more than the people — the particular stubbornness of a sheep that kept drifting, the way a young lamb moved differently from an old one, the sound of the flock settling at dusk. The Storm found her in a museum case. It found her because she had been waiting for a very long time and wanting is a kind of pressure and pressure is a kind of energy and the Storm is very good at finding energy that has nowhere to go.
To the Ones I Remember

You were the stubborn one. The grey one with the notched ear who always drifted left. I knew before you did that you were going to drift — I could feel it in the shift of the wood, the particular angle of your wandering. I never minded. I just came alongside you and you came back and that was how it always went.

I don't know what happened to you. Time did what time does. I was in a case behind glass and you were — elsewhere. A long time ago.

I think about your notched ear sometimes. I think about the way the morning light came across the grass when we were moving and how you were always the first one out and the last one in and I was always there.

I was always there.

To the New Ones

The world is very loud now. I want you to know I noticed that immediately — the glass broke and the noise came in and everything was reversing and I stood in the middle of it and thought: well. This is different.

But the scattered things were still scattered. The drifting things were still drifting. I found you the same way I always found the grey one with the notched ear. I could feel the angle of your wandering.

You are not sheep. I want to be clear that I know this. But the work is the same. Come alongside. Be patient. Be firm when firm is needed.

I will be here when you get back. I always am.

On Returning

I have been asked — once, by someone who found the question very clever — whether it hurts, coming back. It doesn't. It is very simple. I am here and then I am not and then I am here again and the flock needs tending and so I tend it.

What I didn't expect is that the people are not the same people when I return. They have done things while I was gone. The light is different on their faces.

I came back from being glass-case-quiet for centuries and found a world full of people drifting in directions they hadn't chosen and I thought — oh. Oh, I see. This is what I'm for now. Not sheep. People. Same work. Much more interesting.

I'm glad to be here. The dawn is going to come up exactly when it's supposed to. It always does.

The Flock
Memory · Origin
The animals she guided across the steppe for centuries. She remembers them better than she remembers the people — the grey one with the notched ear, the lamb who always drifted left. They are gone. She still feels the weight of their memory in the wood.

The Museum

Where the Storm found her — behind glass in an archive, undignified and waiting. The glass broke. She stepped out. She has not been back.
🌅
The Dawn

Every flock she ever guided moved at first light. The dawn is not something she watches — it is something she has always been part of. She finds it reassuring that it still comes. It always does.
